About us

The Egyptian railways are the first railway lines established in Africa and the Middle East, and the second in the world after the United Kingdom, where construction began in 1834, when the rails of the railway lines were actually extended at the time by the Suez / Alexandria line. However, the work soon stopped due to some political issues, then the idea was revived in 1851 in the fifties of the nineteenth century, to extend across the governorates of Egypt from north to south and from east to west.

The first railway line was extended in Egypt between Cairo / Alexandria. It was built by an English company during the reign of Khedive Abbas I, who took over the rule of Egypt in the year 1848 AD and lasted for six years until 1854 AD. The aim of extending railway lines in Egypt was to facilitate and speed up the transport of mail and travelers between Europe, especially England, and between India in England's largest colonies in the East.

Communications between Europe and India passed through Egypt, as ships come from Europe to the port of Alexandria, and then are transported by land. To Cairo, and from there to the port of Suez, to travel by sea in the Red Sea and then the Indian Ocean to reach India, where the English company hired Robert Stephenson, the son of the inventor of the steam train, to set up this project, which is the first of its kind in the Arab East, where Khedive Abbas I signed a contract With Robert Stephenson, at a value of 56,000 English pounds, to construct a railway linking the Egyptian capital and Alexandria with a length of 209 kilometers.
